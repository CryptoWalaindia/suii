BITCOIN ADDRESS

Series 1: Bitcoin Basics

Bitcoin Address

Definition

A Bitcoin address is a string of letters and numbers used to receive Bitcoin. It works like a
destination for a Bitcoin transaction. When someone wants to send you Bitcoin, you give them a
Bitcoin address from your wallet.

Simple Meaning

A Bitcoin address is like an account destination for receiving Bitcoin.
But it is not exactly the same as a bank account number.
A bank account is linked to a bank and your identity. A Bitcoin address is created by a wallet
and is used on the Bitcoin network.
You can create many Bitcoin addresses from one wallet.
Bitcoin.org recommends using a new Bitcoin address each time you receive a new payment for
better privacy.

Why It Matters

Bitcoin addresses matter because they tell the network where Bitcoin should be sent.
If the address is wrong, the Bitcoin may be lost.
If the address belongs to someone else, they may receive the Bitcoin.
If the address is copied incorrectly, the transaction may fail or go to the wrong place.
This is why checking addresses is an important habit.

How It Works

A Bitcoin wallet creates addresses using cryptographic keys.
The user shares the address with someone who wants to send Bitcoin. The sender enters that
address into their wallet and creates a transaction.
The Bitcoin network then checks whether the transaction follows the rules.
A Bitcoin address is connected to public-key cryptography, but beginners do not need to
understand all the mathematics at first.

Real-World Example

Asha wants Rahul to send her Bitcoin.
She opens her Bitcoin wallet and taps “Receive.”
The wallet shows a Bitcoin address and QR code.
Asha shares it with Rahul.
Rahul scans the QR code and sends Bitcoin to that address.

Common Misunderstanding

Many beginners think one wallet has only one Bitcoin address.
Actually, one wallet can create many addresses. Using a fresh address for each payment can
improve privacy.

FAQs
Is a Bitcoin address the same as a public key?
No. A Bitcoin address is related to public-key cryptography, but it is not exactly the same as a public key. Can I reuse a Bitcoin address? Technically yes, but using a new address for each payment is better for privacy. What happens if I send Bitcoin to the wrong address? Bitcoin transactions are generally not reversible, so the Bitcoin may be lost or sent to someone else. Can someone steal my Bitcoin with only my address? No. A Bitcoin address can be shared to receive Bitcoin. The private key or seed phrase must stay secret. Why do Bitcoin addresses look complicated? They are designed for cryptographic accuracy, not human memory. Most people use copy-paste or QR codes.
