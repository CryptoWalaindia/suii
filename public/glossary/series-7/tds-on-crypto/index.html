TDS on Crypto

Quick Answer

TDS on crypto in India generally refers to 1% tax deducted at source under Section 194S on
payments made to a resident for the transfer of a Virtual Digital Asset, subject to specified
thresholds.

Definition

TDS on crypto is the tax deducted at source when certain payments are made for the transfer of
Virtual Digital Assets under Indian income tax rules.

Simple Meaning

TDS means tax is deducted before the seller receives the full payment.
In crypto, this was introduced to create transaction reporting and tax compliance.
It does not mean the government is charging only 1% total tax.
It means 1% may be deducted at source and reported.

Why It Matters

TDS matters because it affects cash flow and tax reporting.
If you trade frequently, TDS can add up across transactions.
It also creates a record that may appear in your tax documents.
This is why beginners should track all transactions carefully.
Section 194S in Simple Words
Section 194S deals with TDS on payment for transfer of Virtual Digital Assets.
Income Tax Department guidance states:
● 1% TDS applies when payment is made to a resident for transfer of VDA
● No TDS is required if payment does not exceed ₹50,000 for specified persons
● No TDS is required if payment does not exceed ₹10,000 for other payers
These thresholds and rules should be checked before filing or transacting because tax rules can
change.

Real-World Example

Asha sells crypto worth ₹20,000 on a platform.
Depending on the platform, transaction structure, and applicable rules, TDS may be deducted
and reported.
This TDS is not necessarily her final tax. She still needs to calculate total income and report
correctly.

Common Misunderstanding

Many beginners think:
“1% TDS means my crypto tax is done.”
That is not correct.
TDS is only a deduction at source. Final tax may be different depending on gains, income, and
reporting.

FAQs
What is TDS on crypto?
TDS on crypto is tax deducted at source on certain VDA transfers. What is the TDS rate on crypto in India? Income Tax Department guidance states 1% TDS under Section 194S for payments to residents for transfer of VDA, subject to thresholds. Is TDS the same as income tax? No. TDS is deducted at source. Final tax calculation is separate. Does TDS apply to every crypto transaction? It depends on transaction type, amount, parties involved, and applicable thresholds. Where can I see deducted TDS? It may appear in tax records such as Form 26AS or AIS, depending on reporting.
