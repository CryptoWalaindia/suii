CBDC

Quick Answer

CBDC stands for Central Bank Digital Currency. It is digital money issued by a country’s central
bank. India’s CBDC is called the Digital Rupee or e₹.

Definition

A CBDC is a digital form of central bank money issued and backed by a country’s central bank.

Simple Meaning

A CBDC is government-backed digital money.
It is not a private cryptocurrency.
It is not created by a decentralized network.
It is issued by a central bank, such as RBI in India.

Why It Matters

CBDCs matter because central banks around the world are studying how money should work in
a digital economy.
People already use digital payments every day.
But most digital payments today move bank deposits or payment balances.
A CBDC is different because it represents central bank money in digital form.
RBI’s FAQ describes Digital Rupee as India’s CBDC and says it is a digital form of the rupee
banknote.
Types of CBDC
CBDCs are usually discussed in two types:
Retail CBDC
Used by individuals and businesses for payments.
Wholesale CBDC
Used by financial institutions for settlement.
RBI’s Digital Rupee FAQ discusses both retail and wholesale CBDC.
CBDC vs Bitcoin
CBDC and Bitcoin are both digital, but they are very different.
CBDC:
Issued by central bank
Legal tender
Controlled monetary system
Usually permissioned
National currency
Bitcoin:
No central issuer
Global network
Fixed supply
Open participation
Decentralized verification

Real-World Example

Digital Rupee is India’s CBDC.
If RBI issues e₹ through participating banks, users may hold and use it through approved
wallets.
This is different from holding Bitcoin in a self-custody wallet.

Common Misunderstanding

Many beginners think CBDC means cryptocurrency.
Not exactly.
CBDC uses digital technology, but it is central bank money. Bitcoin is decentralized crypto
money. They are not the same.

FAQs
What does CBDC stand for?
CBDC stands for Central Bank Digital Currency. What is India’s CBDC called? India’s CBDC is called Digital Rupee or e₹. Is CBDC the same as Bitcoin? No. CBDC is issued by a central bank. Bitcoin is decentralized. Is CBDC legal tender? A CBDC can be legal tender depending on the country’s design. RBI says India’s e₹ is legal tender. Why are central banks exploring CBDCs? They are exploring digital money, payment efficiency, settlement, innovation, and policy use cases.
