Series 7: Advanced Bitcoin Concepts

RBI and Crypto

Quick Answer

RBI has generally taken a cautious view of private cryptocurrencies because of concerns
around financial stability, consumer protection, money laundering, and monetary control. At the
same time, RBI supports India’s own central bank digital currency, the Digital Rupee.

Definition

RBI and crypto refers to the Reserve Bank of India’s position, warnings, policy concerns, and
regulatory approach toward private cryptocurrencies and digital assets.

Simple Meaning

RBI is India’s central bank.
It manages important parts of the financial system, including:
● Monetary policy
● Currency issuance
● Banking regulation
● Financial stability
● Payment systems
Because crypto assets can affect money, payments, and financial behaviour, RBI pays close
attention to them.
RBI’s Main Concerns
RBI has repeatedly warned users about risks in virtual currencies.
RBI’s earlier caution note on virtual currencies highlighted risks related to digital wallets,
hacking, loss of password, malware, absence of authorized central agency, volatility, and
legal/customer protection concerns.
RBI’s concerns usually include:
● Consumer protection
● Fraud and scams
● Market volatility
● Money laundering risk
● Financial stability
● Currency and monetary policy risk
● Lack of underlying issuer in private crypto assets
RBI and Digital Rupee
RBI is cautious about private cryptocurrencies, but it supports Digital Rupee.
Digital Rupee is different because it is issued by RBI itself.
RBI’s FAQ says e₹ is India’s CBDC, a digital form of the rupee banknote, legal tender, and RBI’s
liability.
Reuters reported in May 2026 that RBI planned to expand Digital Rupee use cases, including
welfare schemes and cross-border payment pilots, based on its 2025–26 annual report.
RBI View vs Indian Tax Rules
Beginners should understand this clearly:
RBI’s caution on crypto is one thing.
Income tax rules on VDAs are another thing.
Taxation does not mean RBI treats Bitcoin like rupee.
It only means income from VDA transfers is taxable under Indian law.

Real-World Example

Asha hears that crypto is taxed in India.
She assumes RBI approves crypto like it approves rupee payments.
That is not correct.
India can tax VDA income while RBI still warns about risks in private crypto assets.

Common Misunderstanding

Many people think RBI and the Government of India have exactly the same role.
They do not.
RBI is the central bank.
The government makes laws and tax policy.
Regulators and agencies handle different parts of compliance.
Crypto regulation can involve multiple institutions.

FAQs
What is RBI’s view on crypto?
RBI has generally taken a cautious view and has warned users about risks in private virtual currencies. Does RBI issue Bitcoin? No. Bitcoin is not issued by RBI or any central bank. Does RBI issue Digital Rupee? Yes. Digital Rupee is India’s CBDC issued by RBI. Is Digital Rupee the same as crypto? No. Digital Rupee is central bank money. Private crypto assets like Bitcoin are different. Does crypto tax mean RBI approves crypto? No. Taxation and central bank approval are different issues.
