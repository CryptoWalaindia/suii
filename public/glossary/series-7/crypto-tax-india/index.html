Crypto Tax in India

Quick Answer

Crypto tax in India refers to the tax rules applied to income from Virtual Digital Assets such as
cryptocurrencies. As per Income Tax Department guidance, income from transfer of VDAs is
taxed at 30%, with no deduction except cost of acquisition, and 1% TDS may apply under
Section 194S subject to thresholds.

Definition

Crypto tax in India is the tax treatment of income, gains, and transactions involving Virtual
Digital Assets under Indian income tax law.

Simple Meaning

If you make income from selling or transferring crypto in India, tax rules may apply.
The Indian tax system uses the term Virtual Digital Asset, or VDA.
This can include cryptocurrencies and certain digital tokens.
For beginners, the key point is simple:
Crypto profits are not ignored by the tax system.

Why It Matters

Crypto tax matters because many beginners focus only on price.
But taxes can affect the final outcome.
If someone buys Bitcoin and later sells it at a gain, they need to understand how that gain may
be taxed.
If TDS is deducted, they need to know where it appears and how to report it.
If they make a loss, they need to understand current loss set-off restrictions.
Current Basic Tax Treatment
According to Income Tax Department material, income from transfer of VDA is taxable at 30%.
The guidance also states that no deduction is allowed except the cost of acquisition, and no
set-off of loss is allowed.
This means beginners should not assume crypto is taxed like normal equity investments or
mutual funds.
TDS on Crypto
TDS means Tax Deducted at Source.
For VDA transfers, Section 194S can apply. Income Tax Department guidance states that 1%
TDS applies when payment is made to a resident for transfer of VDA, subject to thresholds.
This does not mean TDS is the final tax. It is tax deducted and reported. Final tax depends on
the income calculation.

Real-World Example

Rahul buys Bitcoin for ₹50,000.
Later, he sells it for ₹70,000.
His gain is ₹20,000 before considering applicable tax treatment.
Under VDA tax rules, the gain may be taxed at 30%, subject to applicable surcharge and cess.
TDS may also apply depending on the transaction.
Rahul should keep records and consult a tax professional.

Common Misunderstanding

Many beginners think 1% TDS means only 1% tax.
That is wrong.
TDS is not the same as final tax. It is a deduction at source. Final tax may still be calculated
separately.

FAQs
What is crypto tax in India?
Crypto tax in India refers to tax rules on income from transfer of Virtual Digital Assets. What is the tax rate on crypto in India? Income Tax Department guidance says income from transfer of VDA is taxable at 30%, with applicable surcharge and cess. Is 1% TDS the final crypto tax? No. TDS is not the final tax. It is tax deducted at source. Can crypto losses be set off in India? Income Tax Department guidance says no set-off of loss is allowed for income from transfer of VDA. Should I consult a CA for crypto tax? Yes. Crypto tax can be complex, especially if you have multiple trades, wallets, exchanges, or cross-border transactions.
