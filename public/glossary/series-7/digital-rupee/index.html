DIGITAL RUPEE

Series 7: Bitcoin in India, Tax & Digital Rupee

Digital Rupee

Quick Answer

Digital Rupee, or e₹, is India’s central bank digital currency issued by the Reserve Bank of India.
It is a digital form of the rupee banknote and is legal tender.

Definition

Digital Rupee is India’s CBDC, a digital version of the Indian rupee issued as a liability of the
Reserve Bank of India.
RBI’s Digital Rupee FAQ says e₹ is a digital form of the rupee banknote, is legal tender, and is
the liability of RBI.

Simple Meaning

Digital Rupee is the rupee in digital form, issued by RBI.
It is not Bitcoin.
It is not a private cryptocurrency.
It is not a bank deposit in the same way as normal bank balance.
It is central bank money in digital form.

Why It Matters

Digital Rupee matters because it shows India is experimenting with the future of money and
payments.
India already has strong digital payments through UPI.
Digital Rupee is different because it is issued directly by the central bank as digital legal tender.
The RBI retail digital rupee pilot began on December 1, 2022, within a closed user group of
participating customers and merchants.
Digital Rupee vs UPI
UPI is a payment system that moves money between bank accounts.
Digital Rupee is money itself in digital form.
Simple comparison:
UPI = payment rail
Digital Rupee = digital central bank money
This distinction is important.
Digital Rupee vs Bitcoin
Digital Rupee is issued by RBI.
Bitcoin is not issued by any central bank.
Digital Rupee is legal tender in India.
Bitcoin is a decentralized digital asset, not Indian legal tender.
Digital Rupee supply and design are controlled by RBI.
Bitcoin supply follows protocol rules.

Real-World Example

If Asha pays a shopkeeper through UPI, money moves between bank accounts.
If she pays using Digital Rupee, she uses RBI-issued digital currency through a supported
wallet or system.
Both may feel digital, but the underlying structure is different.

Common Misunderstanding

Many beginners think Digital Rupee is India’s Bitcoin.
It is not.
Digital Rupee is a central bank digital currency. Bitcoin is decentralized and global.

FAQs
What is Digital Rupee?
Digital Rupee, or e₹, is India’s CBDC issued by RBI. Is Digital Rupee legal tender? Yes. RBI says e₹ is legal tender and a liability of RBI. Is Digital Rupee the same as UPI? No. UPI is a payment system. Digital Rupee is digital central bank money. Is Digital Rupee the same as Bitcoin? No. Digital Rupee is issued by RBI. Bitcoin is decentralized and not issued by a central bank. Can everyone use Digital Rupee? Access depends on RBI pilots, participating banks, wallets, and rollout status.
