21 MILLION BITCOIN SUPPLY

Series 5: Bitcoin Economics & Money

21 Million Bitcoin Supply

Quick Answer

The 21 million Bitcoin supply means Bitcoin has a maximum limit of 21 million coins. This limit is
part of Bitcoin’s rules and is one of the main reasons Bitcoin is studied as scarce digital money.

Definition

The 21 million Bitcoin supply is Bitcoin’s programmed maximum supply limit. Under Bitcoin’s
current consensus rules, no more than 21 million Bitcoin can exist.

Simple Meaning

Bitcoin has a supply cap.
That means there is a maximum number of Bitcoin that can ever be created.
The number is 21 million.
This does not mean all 21 million Bitcoin already exist. New Bitcoin enters circulation through
mining, and the rate of new issuance reduces over time.

Why It Matters

The 21 million supply matters because it makes Bitcoin different from many other forms of
money.
Fiat currency supply can increase over time through central bank and government policy.
Bitcoin’s supply schedule is not decided in meetings. It follows protocol rules.
This is one reason people compare Bitcoin to scarce assets like gold.
Bitcoin.org states that the number of new Bitcoin created each year is automatically halved over
time until issuance stops with a total of 21 million Bitcoin.

How It Works

Bitcoin supply works through mining rewards.
Miners receive newly issued Bitcoin when they add valid blocks. This new issuance is called the
block subsidy.
Over time, the subsidy is cut in half through Bitcoin halving events.
This process continues until new Bitcoin issuance eventually stops.

Real-World Example

Imagine a game where only 21 million rare cards can ever exist.
The rules of the game do not allow more cards to be created.
Bitcoin’s 21 million supply cap works in a similar way. The limit is part of the system’s rules.

Common Misunderstanding

Some beginners think the 21 million limit means one full Bitcoin is the smallest unit.
That is not true.
Bitcoin is divisible. One Bitcoin can be divided into 100 million satoshis. This means people can
use small fractions of Bitcoin.

FAQs
Will there only ever be 21 million Bitcoin?
Under Bitcoin’s current rules, yes. Bitcoin has a maximum supply limit of 21 million coins. Are all 21 million Bitcoin already mined? No. New Bitcoin continues to enter circulation through mining rewards, but the issuance rate reduces over time. Can Bitcoin’s 21 million cap change? Changing it would require broad network agreement. Such a change would be highly controversial because the supply cap is central to Bitcoin’s identity. Does the supply cap guarantee Bitcoin’s price? No. A fixed supply does not guarantee price movement. Demand and market conditions also matter. Can people own less than one Bitcoin? Yes. Bitcoin can be divided into satoshis, the smallest unit of Bitcoin.
